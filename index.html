About Good Universe

Built on belief.
Driven by dignity.

Good Universe was founded with a simple but powerful belief that access to knowledge, dignity, and opportunity should never be determined by gender, geography, or circumstance.

Over a decade, that belief has shaped everything we do. Our work sits at the intersection of menstrual health, gender equity, climate action, education, and skilling with a focus on building solutions that are both practical and sustainable. We engage with communities through awareness, training, research, and on-ground implementation, ensuring that information is not only shared but understood, applied, and carried forward.

At the heart of our approach is the idea that change begins with people. When individuals are equipped with the right tools and knowledge, they are able to make informed choices, challenge existing norms, and influence the spaces around them. This is how small interventions grow into lasting impact. Collaboration continues to be central to our work partnering with institutions, governments, schools, and community networks to remain responsive to real needs while building systems that support long-term, generational change.

Adolescent Wellbeing program

Four pillars.
One unstoppable girl.

In a context where only 57.6% of young women in rural India use hygienic menstrual protection and nearly 23 million girls drop out of school annually due to menstruation-related challenges intervention is not optional. It is urgent.

18,000+
Adolescent girls reached through the AWP in 2025–26
The Adolescent Well-Being program is a bold, school-based intervention built on four transformative pillars: sustainable sexual and reproductive health education, mental health literacy, nutrition awareness, and life skills development delivered across government schools in Telangana and beyond.
5 States
110 Government Schools
500+ Community Women
Pillar 01
Menstrual Health & Reproductive Education
Demystifying menstruation through scientific, shame-free education. Promoting eco-friendly menstrual practices. Before intervention as few as 12% of girls could explain menstruation. After our intervention, it increased to 91%. Not just knowledge. The shift was in how they held themselves.
Pillar 02
Mental Health Literacy
Building emotional vocabulary, resilience, and help-seeking behaviour in classrooms where these conversations have never happened. Before 9.7% correctly defined mental health. After our intervention, it increased to 89%. Confusion transformed into clarity. Isolation into dialogue.
Pillar 03
Nutrition & Anaemia Awareness
Addressing the crisis that two-thirds of Indian adolescent girls aged 10–19 are anaemic directly impacting energy, attendance, and academic performance. Before 16% understood anaemia. After our intervention, it increased to 91%. Knowledge that protects bodies and secures futures.
Pillar 04
Life Skills & Self-Belief
Structured sessions on self-worth, boundary-setting, and confidence. Before 9.9% felt very confident in their strengths. After our intervention, it increased to 88%. Before 17.9% felt comfortable saying no. After our intervention, it increased to 83%.. When empowerment becomes measurable, it becomes real.

Healthcare & Community Development

Health is the foundation
upon which everything else is built.

In many communities, healthcare is still reactive rather than preventive. The need, therefore, is not only for treatment facilities but for structured, community-centered health interventions that prioritize awareness, early detection, and preventive care. Strengthening community health is not auxiliary work. It is central to everything we do.

PHC+
